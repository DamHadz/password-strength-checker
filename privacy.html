Privacy Policy

Last updated: January 1, 2026 · Effective immediately

🔒 Short version: PassGuard never collects, transmits, or stores your passwords. All analysis runs 100% inside your browser. We have no server-side processing of password data whatsoever.

01 Who We Are

PassGuard ("we", "us", "our") is a free online tool that helps users evaluate the strength of their passwords. This Privacy Policy explains how we handle information when you visit passguard.com.

02 What We Do NOT Collect

We want to be crystal clear about this:

  • We never collect, log, or transmit the passwords you type into the checker.
  • We do not store any personally identifiable information (PII).
  • We do not have user accounts, login systems, or profile data.
  • We do not sell, rent, or share any data with third parties for marketing purposes.
  • We do not use tracking cookies beyond what is described in section 04 below.

All password strength analysis is performed entirely within your browser using JavaScript. No network request is ever made when you type or test a password.

03 Information Automatically Collected

Like most websites, our hosting provider (Vercel) may automatically collect standard server log information when you visit, including:

  • Your IP address (anonymised after 24 hours)
  • Browser type and version
  • The page you requested and the time of visit
  • Referring URL (what site linked you here)

This data is used solely for security monitoring and basic traffic analytics. It is never linked to password activity.

04 Analytics & Advertising

Google Analytics 4: We use Google Analytics to understand aggregate usage patterns (e.g. how many users visit, which countries they're from, which features they use). Google Analytics collects anonymised behavioural data. You can opt out using the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on.

Google AdSense: We display ads served by Google AdSense to help fund the free service. Google may use cookies to serve ads based on your interests. You can manage ad personalisation at adssettings.google.com.

We have configured these services to not share any password-related event data with Google. Events tracked are limited to general tool usage (e.g. "password generated", "strength checked") — no password content is included.

05 Cookies

We use minimal cookies for the following purposes:

  • Theme preference: A localStorage entry stores your dark/light mode preference. This stays on your device only and is not sent to any server.
  • Analytics cookies: Set by Google Analytics for aggregate traffic measurement.
  • Advertising cookies: Set by Google AdSense for ad personalisation (optional).

You can disable cookies in your browser settings at any time. The password checker tool will continue to function normally without cookies.

06 Third-Party Services

We use the following third-party services, each with their own privacy policies:

  • Vercel — Hosting and content delivery
  • Google Analytics — Usage analytics
  • Google AdSense — Advertising
  • Google Fonts — Typography (Outfit, Plus Jakarta Sans)

07 Data Security

Because we process no sensitive data on our servers, the security risk is minimal. However, the site is served over HTTPS with strict security headers including X-Content-Type-Options, X-Frame-Options, and X-XSS-Protection.

Your passwords never leave your device — this is the strongest possible data security guarantee we can offer.

08 Children's Privacy

PassGuard does not knowingly collect information from children under the age of 13. If you believe a child has provided personal information through this site, please contact us so we can remove it promptly.

09 Your Rights (GDPR / CCPA)

If you are located in the European Economic Area or California, you have the right to:

  • Access any personal data we hold about you (we hold none password-related)
  • Request deletion of any personal data
  • Opt out of analytics and advertising tracking
  • Lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority

Since we hold no user accounts or password data, most requests can be satisfied by simply clearing your browser cookies and local storage.

10 Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Changes will be reflected by an updated "Last updated" date at the top of this page. We encourage you to review this policy periodically.

Questions about this policy?

If you have any questions or concerns about our privacy practices, we're happy to help.
